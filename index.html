Taking a controlled approach to contemporary abstract art

I have been developing this style of artwork since 1998. It all started with the rise in popularity of tribal tattoo art at around the same time. I liked the way it looked but wanted to put my own spin on it. If I'm being honest.. I couldn't get the mirroring effect right and out of frustration just started to create random line work. It was fun because I could interpret figures and animals, insects, etc out of the finished works. Wanting to figure out a way to elevate the style I decided to try and completely fill the artboards to see how it turned out. I loved the results and have been trying to expand on my style ever since!

Black & White Artwork

It was around 2011 that began to experiment with expanding my tribal art style to fill entire artboard surfaces. I played around with line weights and scale to create depth and more interesting compositions. I worked hard to try and control the movement of the viewers eyes accross my work.

Color Artwork

After getting feedback from fans that they were wondering how the art would look if I added in some colour I decided to give it shot. My studies in brand design also led me the idea of niching down and working in images of street dancers in an effort to spread a more positive brand message.
